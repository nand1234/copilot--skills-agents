# Business Interaction Workflow

## Overview

This document outlines the standard process for managing business interactions — from initial contact through resolution and follow-up.

---

## Participants

- **Initiator** – The person or system that starts the interaction (e.g., customer, partner, internal team).
- **Receiver** – The person or team that receives and processes the interaction.
- **Stakeholder** – Any party with interest in the outcome.
- **Approver** – Decision-maker for escalated or high-impact interactions.

---

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Initiation
- The **Initiator** submits a request, inquiry, or issue via a defined channel (email, portal, phone, etc.).
- The system or receiver acknowledges receipt and assigns a reference ID.

**Output:** Interaction record created with status `Open`.

---

### Step 2: Triage & Assignment
- The **Receiver** reviews the interaction and categorizes it:
  - Type: Request / Complaint / Inquiry / Escalation
  - Priority: Low / Medium / High / Critical
- The interaction is assigned to the appropriate team or individual.

**Output:** Interaction assigned with priority and category.

---

### Step 3: Analysis & Response Preparation
- The assigned party investigates and gathers relevant information.
- If additional input is needed, the **Initiator** is contacted for clarification.
- A response or resolution is drafted.

**Output:** Draft response or action plan ready.

---

### Step 4: Review & Approval (if required)
- For high-priority or high-impact interactions, the draft is reviewed by an **Approver**.
- Approver either:
  - **Approves** → proceed to Step 5.
  - **Requests changes** → return to Step 3.

**Output:** Approved response or action plan.

---

### Step 5: Response Delivery
- The final response or resolution is communicated to the **Initiator**.
- All relevant **Stakeholders** are notified as appropriate.

**Output:** Interaction status updated to `Resolved` or `In Progress` (if action is ongoing).

---

### Step 6: Confirmation & Closure
- The **Initiator** confirms whether the response/resolution is satisfactory.
  - **Satisfied** → Interaction is closed.
  - **Not Satisfied** → Interaction is escalated (return to Step 2 with higher priority).

**Output:** Interaction status updated to `Closed` or `Escalated`.

---

### Step 7: Follow-Up & Documentation
- A follow-up is scheduled (if applicable) to ensure resolution effectiveness.
- The interaction record is documented with key details:
  - Root cause
  - Actions taken
  - Time to resolution
- Insights are shared with relevant teams for continuous improvement.

**Output:** Interaction archived; lessons learned captured.

---

## Interaction Status Lifecycle

```
Open → Assigned → In Progress → Resolved → Closed
                                         ↘ Escalated → Assigned (repeat)
```

---

## Key Principles

- **Timely Response:** Acknowledge all interactions within the agreed SLA.
- **Clear Communication:** Keep all parties informed at each stage.
- **Accountability:** Every interaction must have a clear owner.
- **Continuous Improvement:** Use interaction data to identify patterns and prevent recurring issues.
